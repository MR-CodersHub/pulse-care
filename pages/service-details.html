Clinical Guideline Sheet

Seasonal Flu Immunizations Guide

1. What is this service?

This service provides professional, mobile administration of FDA-approved, preservative-free quadrivalent influenza vaccines. Every clinic visit includes a standard physical pre-screening, vaccine drawing, and patient injection administered directly by a board-certified Registered Nurse (RN).

2. Who is it for?

Annual immunizations are recommended by the CDC for everyone 6 months and older. PulseCare provides in-home nurse visits for pediatric patients (ages 4+), adults, families, and high-dose senior immunizations (65+), as well as coordinate corporate wellness programs for office groups.

3. Why is it beneficial?

Our mobile service reduces clinical exposure by eliminating clinic waiting rooms. Efficacy ranges from 40% to 60% according to the CDC, significantly decreasing the probability of severe respiratory complications, secondary bacterial pneumonias, and hospitalizations during high-contagion months.

4. How does it work?

Registered Nurses transport vaccines using continuous IoT data-logged coolers to ensure a strict 2°C to 8°C cold chain. Prior to vaccine draw, the nurse performs a health history pre-screen and records patient baseline vitals. Following the deltoid injection, a mandatory 15-minute observation window is held to guarantee patient safety.

Pre-Visit Checklist: Hydrate fully before the nurse arrives, wear loose deltoid-exposing attire, consume a light meal, and prepare identification.

5. How can I access it?

Access the service by scheduling through our online dispatch page, completing the secure health consent questionnaire, and selecting an open arrival timeframe for your region.

Clinical Safety, Side Effects & Contraindications

What are the common vaccine side effects?

Typical localized side effects include soreness, redness, or minor swelling at the injection site. Mild systemic symptoms, such as a low-grade fever, headache, or muscle fatigue, represent normal immune activation and typically resolve within 24 to 48 hours without intervention.

Who should not receive the flu vaccination?

Individuals with a history of severe, life-threatening allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) to influenza vaccine ingredients should consult their physician. If you currently have an active, high-grade systemic fever, we recommend rescheduling your mobile appointment until your temperature returns to baseline.

Wellness & Hydration Guide

At-Home Wellness Assessments & Drip Therapies

1. What is this service?

An on-site healthcare visit combining professional physical screening (resting pulse, blood pressure, oxygen saturation levels) with high-efficiency intramuscular (IM) Vitamin B12 booster injections or isotonic IV hydration drip therapies.

2. Who is it for?

Vitals screening and B12 boosters are available for patients of all ages looking to support metabolic rate and immune integrity. IV hydration therapies are restricted to adults aged 18 to 65 who have normal blood pressure and kidney clearance values.

3. Why is it beneficial?

Bypasses standard gastrointestinal absorption degradation to achieve 100% cellular uptake. IV hydration restores fluid volumes, replenishes depleted electrolytes, and clears muscle fatigue by delivering saline directly into venous pathways.

4. How does it work?

A licensed Registered Nurse evaluates baseline vitals before any administration. If vitals confirm safe ranges, the nurse administers the B12 booster into the deltoid muscle or initiates the IV catheter using aseptic techniques. The nurse remains present to monitor the flow and verify safety.

Clinical Safety Boundaries: IV hydration is withheld if blood pressure values are abnormally high or low, or if there is a medical history of cardiovascular disease, congestive health failure, or kidney impairment.

5. How can I access it?

Select the Wellness visit package in our scheduling scheduler, fill out the patient health history records, and book your home nurse slot.

Billing Guide

Insurance Guidelines & Superbill Claims Guide

Under the preventative care mandate of the Affordable Care Act (ACA), annual influenza immunizations are categorized as essential preventative services and are covered at 100% by almost all commercial health insurance plans, with zero out-of-pocket copays or deductibles for eligible patients.

Standard Medical Codes Used

PulseCare issues a detailed medical receipt (Superbill) for every preventative visit. This receipt utilizes standard healthcare code sets recognized by major US insurance companies:

  • ICD-10-CM Diagnostic Code Z23: The official international diagnostic code representing an encounter for immunization services.
  • CPT Procedure Code 90686: Represents the quadrivalent influenza vaccine formulation (preservative-free, 0.5 mL dosage, for intramuscular use).
  • CPT Administration Code 90471: The code representing the physical intramuscular administration of a single vaccine dose by a clinician.
  • NPI & Tax ID: Contains the National Provider Identifier and Tax Identification Number of our mobile nursing clinic group.

Filing Your Reimbursement Claim: Step-by-Step

To file your out-of-network preventative care claim and secure reimbursement, follow these steps:

  1. Download Superbill: Access your secure PulseCare patient portal dashboard to download your PDF Superbill within 24 hours of your visit.
  2. Access Insurer Portal: Log in to your health insurance provider's online member portal (e.g., Aetna, Cigna, BCBS, UnitedHealthcare).
  3. Initiate Claim: Go to the "Submit a Claim" or "Member Reimbursement Form" tab and input the date of service, NPI provider details, and total paid amount.
  4. Attach Documentation: Upload the PDF Superbill copy directly as the proof of preventative treatment.
  5. Track Status: Insurers typical process preventative claims within 10 to 14 business days, mailing a direct reimbursement check or depositing funds online.
Logistics Guide

Active Dispatch Zones & Cold-Chain Logistics

PulseCare mobile units are dispatched systematically across regional service routes to maintain optimal schedule timings and keep vaccine storage perfectly secure from the clinic depot directly to your doorstep.

Zip Code Coverage List

Our active mobile nursing units currently serve metropolitan areas and surrounding suburban zip codes. We waive travel fees entirely for group bookings (5+ individuals) within active dispatch corridors. If booking for a single individual, a standard mileage-based travel fee applies unless combined with an optimized neighborhood route.

Strict Cold-Chain Operations

Preventative vaccines are temperature-sensitive biological structures that rapidly lose effectiveness if exposed to heat or excessive cold. PulseCare follows strict CDC vaccine storage guidelines:

  • Continuous temperature logs: Vaccines are transported in certified portable clinical containers containing IoT data loggers.
  • The 2°C to 8°C Window: Coolers maintain a strict temperature range. If temperatures deviate by even 0.5°C, real-time alerts warn our coordinator.
  • Automatic Rescheduling: In the highly unlikely event of temperature deviation, the batch is safely marked for disposal, and your visit is rescheduled.
Booking Guide

Secure Online Booking & Health Pre-Screening

Patient safety requires careful medical pre-screening. Our clinical booking flow is built to identify pre-existing conditions, allergic history, and consents before a Registered Nurse is dispatched to your location.

Patient Pre-Screening Questionnaire Details

During the booking process, you will complete a secure pre-screening questionnaire covering:

  • History of severe vaccine allergies or anaphylactic episodes.
  • Active infections, acute sickness, or high body temperature conditions.
  • Current medication lists (specifically immunosuppressive drugs or blood thinners).
  • Previous history of Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS) or bleeding disorders.

On-Site Nurse Protocols

Upon arrival, your designated Registered Nurse follows a rigorous clinical checklist:

  1. Vitals check: Measures blood pressure, heart rate, and oximetry to confirm baseline stability.
  2. Identity Verification: Confirms patient ID, selected dosage, and signed medical consents.
  3. Administration: Safely administers vaccine or wellness booster utilizing sterile, single-use syringes.
  4. Observation: Observes the patient for a mandatory 15-minute period to check for any immediate reactions.
