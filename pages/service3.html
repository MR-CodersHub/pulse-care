IV Hydration Drip Therapy

Rapid physiological rehydration with normal saline containing water-soluble vitamins, magnesium, and essential trace minerals.

Duration: 30 - 60 min
Protec: 12 months

Treatment Clinical Overview

The Quadrivalent Influenza Vaccine protects against four distinct strains of influenza: two influenza A viruses and two influenza B viruses. Annual preventative vaccination is recommended by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) for individuals ages 6 months and older to minimize severe clinical symptoms and respiratory risks during winter seasons.

Patient Preparation & Guidelines

To guarantee a smooth, comfortable injection experience, our clinical nurses recommend:

  • Maintain proper physiological hydration prior to nurse arrival.
  • Wear short sleeves or loose clothing enabling easy access to upper deltoid muscles.
  • Have a minor meal or healthy snack within 2 hours of treatment.
  • Disclose active medicine schedules or past allergy symptoms to the nurse.

Clinical Safety Accordion

What are the common side effects?

Mild local deltoid soreness, low-grade muscle tiredness, or local skin redness represents typical immunological reactions that resolve naturally within 24 to 48 hours.

Who should not receive the flu shot?

Individuals with past life-threatening anaphylactic responses to vaccines or active, high-grade systemic fevers should consult doctors prior to immunization scheduling.
